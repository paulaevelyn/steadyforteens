How Steady works

This is not about being “less emotional.” Emotions are information. The goal is to get through the intense part of the wave without hurting yourself, your relationships, or your future self.

The core idea: When feelings are very intense, your body may act as if there is an emergency. Steady helps you lower the alarm first, then decide what to do.

More self-trust

Clearer communication

You can return to the conversation with more honesty and less panic.

Why emotions can feel so intense

A DBT view: emotional intensity often grows from a mix of biology, life experiences, current stress, relationships, sleep, hormones, learning history, and the environment around you. Some nervous systems react faster or more strongly. If your feelings are intense, it does not mean you are broken. It means your system may need more specific skills, more validation, and more practice.
A compassion-focused view: human minds have “bugs.” Our brains evolved to detect threat quickly, imagine worst-case scenarios, compare ourselves, replay painful moments, and treat social disconnection like danger. This is not your fault. But it can become your responsibility to learn how to work with the mind you have — gently and skillfully.
Important: Steady is a support tool, not emergency care. If you might hurt yourself, cannot stay safe, or feel in immediate danger, contact a safe adult or emergency service now.

Skill Map

Choose the skill that fits the moment. You do not need to use all of them. You need the right one for now.

STOP

Best for: “I am about to react.”

Stop. Take a step back. Observe. Proceed mindfully.

TIPP

Best for: body emergency, 8–10/10 intensity.

Temperature, intense movement, paced breathing, muscle relaxation.

Pros & Cons

Best for: urges that could create damage.

Compare short-term relief with long-term consequences.

ACCEPTS

Best for: waiting without spiraling.

Activities, contributing, comparisons, emotions, pushing away, thoughts, sensations.

Self-Soothe

Best for: shame, loneliness, fear, emotional pain.

Use the five senses to signal safety to your nervous system.

Pause Message

Best for: needing space without disappearing.

Communicate pause + care + return time.

Practice a skill to unlock feedback.

Missions

Body First

Use cold water or paced breathing before checking your phone again.

Facts vs Story

Write 2 facts you know and 2 things your mind is guessing.

Safe Pause

Support Plan

Distress tolerance is not the same as handling everything alone. Support is a skill.

Ask for immediate adult help or emergency support if: you might hurt yourself, you have a suicide plan, you feel unsafe, you are being pressured or abused, substances are involved, or you cannot calm down after trying skills.
